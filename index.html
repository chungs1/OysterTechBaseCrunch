Oyster Aims To Make Readers Happy As A Clam

Posted April 8, 2014 by Phil Nachum, Sophia Chung, Nicole Fronda, Lisa Chang, Alex Park
Next Story

On-Demand Laundry Startup Washio Raises $2.25 Million In Seed Funding

New York-based startup Oysteroffers unlimited access to a library of books at an affordable price along with the option to sample without committing. In addition, Oyster offers a simple interface for an appealing reading experience to the user. Although unique in its use of a subscription model, Oyster is only available on iOS devices, which might be one of the major setbacks in comparisons with its competitors. However, it does solve the problem for those that want to read books on their mobile devices but do not want to pay for individual books on Amazon Kindle or Barnes & Noble Nook.

Oyster is targeting avid book readers – particularly those with mobile devices - who are already comfortable with reading eBooks. They hope to target a niche of eBook readers – “powerhouse readers” - who read as a serious hobby or as a living, such as critics, teachers, book club members, or writers.

When a user signs up, they pay a monthly subscription fee of $9.95. However, the way Oyster structures its content deals is intended to be publisher friendly. If a subscriber reads more than 10 percent of a book, the publisher receives 60 percent of the book’s retail list price. However, if a subscriber reads more than three or four books a month, Oyster’s subscription price would not be able to cover the costs of maintaining their library and deals with publishers.

However, Oyster already has a strong user base, and is optimistic that as it grows its library and expands to other platforms, more people will become interested. In terms of focus, its aim is avid readers, such as those who regularly attend book clubs. Statiscally, people often do not have time to read more than 99 minutes a day. However, those who participate in book clubs often make time in their lives and have a community where they share books with others. Oyster provides these people with a simple way to search its catalog and easily find these books, which book clubs thrive upon. From these avid readers and shared networking of book clubs, expansion should naturally follow.

Oyster faces several competitors that provide many of the same features. However, Oyster is confident that although it does not offer the largest library, it does offer the best library out on the market right now. It was first to market with HarperCollins, a Big 5 publisher. Its catalog includes titles from hundreds of well known publishers, including Perseus, Melville House, Rodale, and Smashwords, all of which continue to add new titles frequently. In addition, Oyster is built in New York, which is known as the heart of the publishing world. This gives Oyster the advantage of building robust connections throughout the publishing industry. The geographic proximity to its partners allows the team to work closely and effectively with them, which Oyster believes is the key to providing the best content for its customers.

In the long term, Oyster is looking to expand its library first and foremost, as well as to support Android and other platforms. and adding possible new features such as user reviews, recommendations, and forums.
